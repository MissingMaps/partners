Hosting a Mapathon

A mapathon allows volunteers the opportunity to digitally connect and map the most vulnerable places in the developing world. As Microsoft employees, our work with Missing Maps helps local and international Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) get the data they need to better respond to crises affecting these areas. Up to date maps are important for the success of many humanitarian organizations around the world in responding to disasters. Your time spent volunteering with Missing Maps can have a real impact on the delivery of healthcare in the field.

Get Started

As a mapathon host your role is to plan and execute the event. You will be the one to choose the date, time, and location, invite attendees, invite a facilitator, and follow up with volunteers after the event.

Planning Checklist Event Day Checklist Post Event Checklist Materials
 

Planning Checklist

  • Choose a date and time for your event
  • Recruit volunteers to help plan and facilitate
  • Book a space or Skype call
  • Invite employees and remind them to bring their laptops (required) and mice (optional)
  • Establish registration method (ex. S+, Outlook Voting, Forms, Badge Scanner)
  • Establish method for capturing who attended the event (ex. Setup a registration table using a log sheet)
  • Pick a task from the Microsoft Missing Maps Partner Page
  • Identify a group hashtag to track event progress
  • Print mapathon resources including step-by-step mapping guide and project number and hashtag handout
  • Order snacks (optional)
 

Event Day Checklist

  • Capture attendance information
  • Put out printed resources
  • Load Missing Maps presentation and demo pages for the Microsoft Missing Maps Partner Page and your chosen task
  • Set up snacks
  • Have fun mapping!
 

Post Event Checklist

  • Send recap, thank you mail to attendees
  • Include link to match time for participants who didn’t log at the event
 

Training & Event Materials

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hosting a Mapathon
  • Invitation template
  • Guide to using hashtags and the leaderboard
  • Missing Maps presentation
  • Step-by-step mapping handout
  • Project number and hashtag handout
  • Introduction Video – How to Map

Mapathon Support

The Missing Maps team can support you in several ways. Please reach out to our contacts at the American Red Cross for help with task selection, in-person mapathon facilitation or if you have any questions.

Hosting a Mapathon

 

Before event

  • Choose a date and time for your event, we recommend a 90 minute to 2 hour event as a best practice
  • Confirm an HPE Missing Maps Champion can facilitate your mapping event
  • Register your event with the Red Cross so they can track impact and HPE mapping data
  • Book a space or online meeting via HPE MyRoom
  • Invite employees using the email template
  • Send a reminder 1-2 days before your event, reminding attendees to register a mapping user ID in advance and to bring their laptops (required in person) and mice (optional)
  • Pick a task from the HPE Missing Maps Partner Page
  • Use #HPEGives and consider creating a hashtag for your site or event to track your progress
 

During event

  • Set up presentation technology needed for your HPE Missing Maps Champion facilitator early and fix any audio/visual issues
  • Capture attendance information
  • In-person events: Put out any printed resources, plug in extra power strips, and set up snacks/swag
  • Online events: Share important links like the HPE Missing Maps Partner Page and mapping user registration page in a common chat or online meeting lobby
  • Load Missing Maps presentation and demo pages for the HPE Missing Maps Partner Page and your chosen task
  • Have fun mapping!
 

After event

  • Send recap, thank you mail to attendees
  • Share your experience in the HPE Awards Group
  • Host another mapathon again soon!
